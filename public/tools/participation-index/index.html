2. Identifying "Participation Theater"

Why do leaders simulate participation instead of enabling it? Behavioral economics points to metric gaming, diffusion of responsibility, and legitimacy signaling. Here we outline the systemic manipulation patterns—observable inverse signals where high communication activity masks low structural impact.

Agenda Control

🎭

Restricting the scope of consultation to non-threatening administrative details rather than systemic issues.

Observable Signal: Citizens can vote on park bench colors, but not the municipal zoning laws dictating the park's existence.

Information Asymmetry

🗄️

Providing complex, redacted, or highly technical data formats that effectively preclude informed citizen deliberation.

Observable Signal: Budget datasets provided as 500-page flattened PDFs rather than queryable, open-source databases.

Narrative Substitution

📢

Replacing binding outcomes with PR language centered around "having a voice," "trust," or "community spirit."

Observable Signal: High PR expenditure on consultation campaigns with exactly zero statutory integration of the resulting outputs.

Selection Bias

🎯

Designing engagement formats that heavily favor time-rich, highly educated, or organized special interest groups.

Observable Signal: Consultations held exclusively at 2 PM on Tuesdays; lack of sortition (random selection) mechanisms.

Delayed Implementation

⏳

Using institutional latency to quietly abandon citizen decisions after the immediate PR value has been extracted.

Observable Signal: Time-to-impact latency exceeding electoral cycles; zero independent auditability of execution fidelity.

The Ultimate Metric

⚖️

To pierce the theater, we must measure what is observable: Binding Authority and Resource Allocation.

5. Transition Paths & Safeguards

Moving from low (0-30) to high (70-100) RPGI environments requires structural engineering, not better marketing. These concrete mechanisms act as anti-capture safeguards to ensure power transfer is actualized.

Binding Participatory Budgeting

Requires hardcoded legal thresholds (e.g., minimum 5% of municipal investment budget directly allocated via citizen vote). Bypasses administrative filtering.

Increases: Resource Control, Decision Power

Sortition (Random Selection)

Replacing self-selection with demographically stratified random selection. Eliminates lobbying capture and inclusion bias toward hyper-engaged minorities.

Increases: Inclusiveness, Anti-Capture

Legislative Triggers

Institutional rules mandating that if a citizen assembly reaches a supermajority (e.g., 80%), the parliament *must* debate and vote on the drafted bill within 90 days.

Increases: Accountability, Continuity

Adversarial Audits

Independent verification bodies tasked specifically with tracking the "implementation fidelity" mapping initial citizen decisions to final executed codes/laws.

Increases: Information Symmetry, Accountability
